Lángos (Hungarian fried flatbread)

1h 05m prep / 40m cook / 6 servings /


Topped with garlic, cheese, bacon and sour cream, these crispy fried flatbreads are the ultimate comfort food snack. Don't be put off by homemade dough, this step-by-step recipe makes it foolproof!


Ingredients

  • 250ml (1 cup) milk
  • 1 1/2 tsp dired yeast
  • 1/2 tsp caster sugar
  • 350g (2 1/3 cups) plain flour
  • 1 tsp table salt
  • Vegetable oil, to deep fry
  • Halved garlic cloves, to serve
  • Sour cream, to serve
  • Grated cheddar, to serve
  • Cooked chopped bacon, to serve
  • Chopped fresh parsley leaves, to serve

Directions

Step 1

Warm the milk in the microwave or a saucepan until just lukewarm. Place in a jug (if in the saucepan). Add the yeast and sugar. Use a skewer to stir until combined. Set aside for 5 minutes or until the mixture begins to become frothy.


Step 2

Use a stand mixer with the dough hook attachment to combine flour and salt. Add milk mixture. Beat until just combined. Scrape down side of bowl then continue beating for 3 minutes or until a soft, sticky dough. Grease a bowl with oil. Turn dough onto a lightly oiled surface. Shape into a ball. Place in prepared bowl, cover with plastic wrap and set aside for 45 minutes or until doubled in size.

Step 3

Pour oil into a large wide saucepan until half full. Place over medium-high heat. Heat to 180C on a cook’s thermometer (see tip).


Step 4

Use oiled hands to divide dough into 6 portions. Working with 1 at a time on a lightly oiled surface, roll out or press dough into a 16cm-diameter disc, making it slightly thicker around the edge. Drop gently into oil. Cook for 2-3 minutes each side or until puffed and golden brown. Use tongs to carefully lift out and drain on a plate lined with paper towel. Repeat with the remaining dough portions.


Step 5

Rub warm flatbread with cut garlic. Top with sour cream, cheese, bacon and parsley.
